Customer Segmentation using Clustering
Machine Learning
2025
Pythonscikit-learnPandasMatplotlibSeaborn
Overview

Used a Retail & Product Categorization Datasets , Clustering is applied to group customers based on their behavior, helping businesses identify patterns and drive actionable insights.it was completed as my second project at Infotact Solutions.

Problem Statement

The dataset has a large base but lacks insights into customer behavior, value and purchasing patterns without segmentation, marketing and retention strategies are generic, leading to missed opportunities for targeted promotions, higher sales and improved customer loyalty.

Project Objective

The objective of this project is to clean and preprocess customer transaction data, analyze purchasing behavior using metrics like Income, age, spending score and apply clustering techniques to segment customers based on their patterns and value, enabling better insights through visualization of customer groups.

Steps in Process
Used a Retail & Product Categorization Datasets (200 records) containing demographics, purchase behavior and transactional details for analysis and segmentation.
Performed EDA (univariate and Bivariate) to understand data patterns, relationships and feature distributions.
Identified Annual Income vs Spending Score and Age vs Spending Score as strong features for clustering, while Purchase Frequency vs Loyalty Score was not suitable
Applied the Elbow Method to find optimal clusters (k=5) and performed K-Means Clustering, achieving a Silhouette Score of 71%.
Segmented customers into five distinct groups and interpreted each cluster to derive meaningful business insights.
What I Did
Analyzed sport retail data to understand purchasing behavior and engagement patterns.
Conducted EDA to examine distributions and relationship between key varaibles.
Determined the optimal number of clusters (k=5) using the Elbow Method.
Applied K-Means clustering to segment customers into five distinct groups and validated results using Sihouette Score (0.71)-Excellent Clustering.
Profiled each customer segment to derive actionable business insights.
Outcome
The customer segmentation divided customer based on their transaction behavior and engagement patterns, identifying high value, frequent and occasional groups. The insights from these segments helped guide targeted marketing, personalized offers and startegies for improving customer retention.

Platforms like Facebook generate large volumes of unstructured user data, making analysis difficult. This project focuses on collecting and processing such data to extract insights like trends, sentiment and user engagement patterns, enabling better decision making for businesses and researchers.

The Objective of this project is to develop a system for collecting and analyzing data from Facebook using the Graph API. It focuses on keyword and hashtag trends, sentiment analysis, topic modeling and time based analysis to uncover insights and support better decision making.

Presented results using graphs and charts to make insights clear and support decision making.

College Campaign Data Analysis and Dashboard (SQL/Tableau)
SQLTableauExcel
Overview

Analyzing College Marketing Campaign dataset with 764 recordsusing SQL and Tableau. The goal was to evaluate campaign performance, understand user engagement and provide insights for improving college marketing strategies should cover the entire student journey from admissions to placements, While being more cost effective than before. Metrics such as impression to enrollments based on conversion rate and cost and revenue difference and ROI were analyzed across campaigns.

Problem Statement

Marketing Campaigns generate large amounts of data if the college hold central marketing for campaign but not proper analysis, it is difficult to identify effective campaigns, optimize ROI. This Project focuses on analyzing and visualizing campaign data to deliver insights for improving engagement and performance.

Project Objective

To analyze campaign performance using key metrics like revenue generated, Return on Investment and conversion rate. Revenue helped measure the total income generated from each campaign, while ROI was used to determine the profitability by comparing returns with the cost invested. Conversion rates were analyzed to understand how user interaction (clicks or leads) were turned into actual outcomes like applications or enrollments. By combining these metrics, the Project aim to provide data driven recommendations to improve future marketing strategies.

Steps in Process
Imported the dataset into PostgreSQL for Data storage and Processing.
Performed data inspection and cleaning by handling missing values, duplicates and inconsistencies.
Connected the cleaned data to Tableau for Visualization.
Created Dashboards and calculated key metrics like ROI and conversion rates.
ROI=Revenue-Cost/Cost*100.
Conversion rate=Conversions/Total Clicks*100.
Generated insights and recommendations to improve future campaign performances.
What I Did
Analyzed and processed data using SQL.
Built Interactive dashboards for campaign performance.
Analyzed ROI and conversion rate, Provided clear visualization to improve conversion user interaction.
The analysis revealed that Placement campaigns delvered the highest ROI (24.4%),making them the most profitable.
MBA and UG Admission campaigns showed strong conversion rates which is above 23% indicating effective targeting and lead quality.
Hackathon campaigns underperformed all key metrics with Low ROI(21.1%), low conversion rate (11.82%) and low revenue contribution, which suggest need to optimize or reconsider investment in underperforming campaigns.
Outcome
Evaluated campaign performance by analyzing ROI, revenue and conversion metrics. It revealed the top performing campaign name and channel contributing to higher engagement and results. It also delivered data driven insights through Tableau dashboards to enhance strategy and maximize overall performance.
